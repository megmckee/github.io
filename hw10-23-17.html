Project idea: Charting opiate overdose deaths nationally over time

This CDC data set is available as an API via the Open Data Network, a humungous data repository. I chose this set because it's national in scope -- there are many state-level databases available, too -- and a topic I care about.

There are about 6,500 records, and the variables in the dataset include:

  • State
  • Year
  • Month
  • Indicator, which is the exact type of opiate involved in death
  • The data's percentage of completeness
  • Percent of deaths pending investigation

The data also has a footnote column to document any issues with the data.

The obvious choice for visualization would be a map-based viz. There are also opportunities to look at deaths by time of the year, and clusters where a spat of deaths happened over a short period of time.

Before committing to a design (or designs) I am attending a one-day workshop this Wednesday, Oct. 25, with the amazing viz-master Edward Tufte. One of the best parts is all attendees are given all four of his books. I purposely signed up for this conference knowing it would be invaluable to this class and my project.

Another possibility is overlaying another dataset to see if there are any correlations. For example, the government has a database that looks at Medicare recipients and the rate of opiate prescriptions filled.
